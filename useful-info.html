About the University

Nearly Two Centuries
of Education

The institutional lineage of the University of Wolverhampton traces its origins to the early nineteenth century, beginning with the foundation of the Wolverhampton Tradesmen's and Mechanics' Institute in 1827. Established to provide vocational and general education to the town's working men, this initiative was soon complemented by the opening of the Wolverhampton School of Art in 1851. These early establishments laid the educational groundwork for the region, gradually evolving alongside local industrial needs.

In the twentieth century, these educational streams began to consolidate into larger, formalised structures. The Wolverhampton and Staffordshire Technical College was established in 1926, and Prince George laid the foundation stone for its new major buildings in 1931. Fulfilling a vital role in providing higher scientific and industrial studies, the institution underwent further development and was renamed the Wolverhampton and Staffordshire College of Technology in 1951. The college also demonstrated a pioneering spirit in early computing; in 1957, it won a competition to acquire an early electronic digital computer known as WITCH (Wolverhampton Instrument for Teaching Computation from Harwell), which remained a central teaching tool at the institution until 1973.

A significant structural transformation occurred on 1 September 1969, when the College of Technology merged with the Wolverhampton College of Art to officially form The Polytechnic, Wolverhampton. During the 1970s and 1980s, the Polytechnic experienced considerable growth, notably through amalgamations with regional teacher training institutions. For instance, the Dudley Training College for Teachers, which had operated since 1909, was absorbed into the Polytechnic in 1977.

In 1992, the institution achieved a major academic milestone when it was granted official university status, becoming the University of Wolverhampton. Since acquiring this designation, the university has pursued an ambitious trajectory of expansion and modernisation. Notable developmental milestones include the establishment of the Telford Innovation Campus in 1994, the opening of the University of Wolverhampton Science Park in 1995 in a joint venture with the local council, and the recent inauguration of the £120 million Springfield Campus in 2020 provides the amazing facilities you are in during this Summer School. Today, the University operates across multiple campuses and continues to invest significantly in its academic facilities, student experience, and regional economic development.

Meet the Team

Summer School Staff from the
WLVArchitecture Department

The STEAM Academy 2026 is delivered by academic staff from the School of Architecture, Computing and Engineering at the University of Wolverhampton.

Prof David Heesom
Professor of Digital Built Environment

David Heesom has spent 20 years in academia researching the application of advanced digital technologies for architectural design and construction, publishing his work in international journals and speaking at events around the world. Prior to this he worked as an infrastructure engineer focusing on the design of large highway schemes using both 2D and 3D digital modelling, where his interest in 3D digital modelling began. This work involved both design and site supervision in the UK and the Republic of Ireland. David has won numerous funded research awards and works closely with companies around the UK to support them in implementing BIM and digital technologies to enhance their processes. David is a Chartered Architectural Technologist and Fellow of the CIAT.

Dr Nigel Moore
Lecturer in Architectural Technology

Nigel is an Architectural Technologist (ACIAT) with many years of experience in industry at SME level. Nigel's expertise lies in 3D visualisation and the use of Virtual Reality and Augmented Reality. Nigel holds a PhD in Construction ICT and a Bachelor's Degree in Virtual Reality Design and Visualisation. He has been a lecturer at Wolverhampton since 2021, having previously been a researcher on the Innovate UK STELLAR Project researching and developing parametric design tools, generative design, digital twins and machine learning for modular off-site construction.

Dr Mohammad Tammo
BSc (Hons) Architecture Design Technology Programme Leader

Mo is an architectural designer and educator who has excelled in both architectural practice and teaching at university level. His impressive portfolio includes winning multiple design competitions and publishing research on topics such as Sustainable Design, Sustainable Architecture, and Architectural Technology. Having qualified as an Architect in Syria, Mo holds a Masters in Architecture from the Edinburgh College of Art and a PhD from the University of Bolton in Regeneration and Sustainable Communities, and has been teaching Architecture and Design for 15 years at the Universities of Bolton, Cardiff and Wolverhampton.

Karis Cochrane
Lecturer in Architecture

Karis is an Architect having registered with the ARB in 2013 and has worked in industry since 2005. Karis holds a BA (Hons) from the University of the West of England, an MArch from the University of Bath and a Post Graduate Diploma in Architectural Practice from Birmingham City University. Prior to starting lecturing in 2023, Karis was working in the Heritage sector focusing on the conservation of listed buildings.

Olive White
BSc (Hons) Architecture Programme Leader

Olive is a registered Architect (ARB), achieving her professional membership from the Royal Institute of Architects of Ireland (RIAI). She holds a Diploma in Architecture from Dublin Institute of Technology and a Bachelor of Architectural Science from Trinity College Dublin, as well as an MSc in Urban Design from University College Dublin. Olive brings broad cross-disciplinary experience in both architecture and urban design, having worked on commercial, housing, education, refurbishment, office and master-planning projects in Birmingham, Cork, Dublin and Amsterdam. Olive has served on the Design Review Panel at Birmingham City Council and has been a visiting tutor at Birmingham City University.

Sam Davey
Lecturer in Architecture

Sam is an Architect having registered with the ARB in 2016, and has gained extensive experience in industry since 2007. Sam started lecturing at Wolverhampton in 2022, and holds a BA (Hons) from the University of Westminster, an MArch from Oxford Brookes and a Post Graduate Diploma in Architectural Practice from the University of Greenwich. Sam has a keen interest in architectural model making, having previously worked in the Modelshop and Makerspace at Aston University.
